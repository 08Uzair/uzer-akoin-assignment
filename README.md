# Institute of Digital Risk (IDR) – Branding and Homepage Assignment

## Project Overview

This project presents a simple brand identity and responsive homepage for the Institute of Digital Risk (IDR), an industry-led training and deployment institute focused on digital, cyber, and AI risk. The objective of the assignment was to create a clear and professional brand identity along with a responsive webpage that communicates the institute’s mission, services, and value proposition.

## Logo Design

The IDR logo is based on a cube-inspired geometric structure that represents stability, structure, and layered risk frameworks commonly found in digital and technology risk management. The three faces of the cube symbolize the integration of academic insight, industry practice, and technological innovation.

The colour palette uses orange, black, and white. Orange represents innovation and forward-looking technology, black conveys authority and trust in risk governance, and white reflects clarity and transparency. The minimalist design ensures the logo remains legible and recognizable across different digital environments, including small sizes such as mobile headers and favicons.

Two variants of the logo were created:

* Icon-only version for favicon, mobile headers, and compact branding
* Icon with text “Institute of Digital Risk” for website headers and official branding

## Homepage Design

A responsive single-page website was developed using HTML and CSS to clearly present the institute’s purpose and offerings.

The homepage contains the following sections:

### Hero Section

Introduces the institute with the headline:
“Advancing the Future of Digital Risk.”

It includes a brief description of IDR’s mission and a call-to-action button encouraging users to explore programs.

### About IDR

Explains that IDR is an industry-led institute that trains and deploys professionals in digital, cyber, and technology risk roles. The section highlights the connection between academic collaboration and real-world industry projects.

### Service Pillars

The operational model of IDR is presented through three core pillars:

Academy – Training programs and bootcamps designed to prepare students and professionals for digital risk careers.

Innovation & Incubation – Development of intellectual property, AI governance frameworks, and future risk management models.

Advisory Services – Supporting organizations in implementing risk frameworks such as NIST, ISO, and technology governance standards.

### Footer

Provides basic copyright information and completes the webpage structure.

## Responsive Design

The website layout adapts to both desktop and mobile devices. On larger screens, service pillars are displayed in a horizontal card layout, while on mobile devices they stack vertically for better readability and usability.

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates a clean and professional branding concept along with a structured, responsive web interface for a technology-focused training institute. The design emphasizes clarity, modern aesthetics, and alignment with the themes of digital risk, innovation, and industry collaboration.
